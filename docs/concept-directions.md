# Concept Directions

## Recommended direction first

### Direction A: Systems Atelier

Core idea:

Your portfolio feels like a studio for building complex systems. Not a corporate dashboard, and not a playful toy. More like a carefully designed technical atelier where maps, flows, notes, product artifacts, and experiments live together.

Why it fits:

- Strong match for geospatial work, pipelines, AI features, and architecture-heavy products.
- Lets you feel serious and creative at the same time.
- Supports diagrams, metrics, product decisions, and visual craft naturally.

Visual language:

- Editorial typography with technical annotations
- Layered grids
- Map-like or blueprint-like spatial cues
- Subtle motion that feels precise, not flashy
- Warm industrial or research-lab mood rather than default dark-mode portfolio aesthetics

Possible homepage feeling:

- bold statement
- one living system diagram or spatial canvas
- selected case studies as "systems"
- evidence blocks for impact, architecture, reliability, and product judgment

Best references:

- Antoine Wodniack for authored personality
- Rauno Freiberg for restraint and principle
- Mike Matas for confidence and credibility framing

Risk:

- Could become too abstract if the system metaphor overwhelms the actual story.

### Direction B: Product Field Manual

Core idea:

The site feels like the working notebook of a technical product builder. Each project is documented like an operation manual: context, constraints, architecture, decisions, failures, outcomes.

Why it fits:

- Very strong match for "deep technical engineer who understands product."
- Lets you show thinking, not just output.
- Great for recruiters, founders, and technical leads.

Visual language:

- clean editorial layout
- diagrammatic side notes
- structured callouts
- issue/decision/result blocks
- subtle interaction accents

Possible homepage feeling:

- one thesis statement
- three flagship missions/projects
- principles
- timeline
- selected artifacts

Best references:

- Pedro Duarte for clarity of self-positioning
- Mike Matas for career-story simplicity
- Rauno Freiberg for written principles and detail culture

Risk:

- Could look too document-like if the art direction is not strong enough.

### Direction C: Product Worlds

Core idea:

Instead of one neutral portfolio shell, each featured project feels like entering a distinct product world. The homepage acts like a portal between these worlds.

Why it fits:

- Good if your projects are visually and technically different enough.
- Lets you express creativity through each case study.
- Strong for making the portfolio memorable.

Visual language:

- bold case-study covers
- differentiated palettes
- immersive transitions
- product-specific motion and sound moments

Possible homepage feeling:

- highly visual entry point
- project worlds as destinations
- each world reveals system depth once entered

Best references:

- Studio Null for atmosphere
- Bruno Simon for world-building mindset
- Gregory Lalle for curation and pacing

Risk:

- More complex to design and build.
- Easier to become style-first and less coherent.

## My recommendation

If we want the strongest balance of:

- engineering credibility
- product intelligence
- memorable design
- room for creativity

then **Direction A: Systems Atelier** is the best first concept to develop.

If we want the clearest and most hiring-friendly version, choose **Direction B: Product Field Manual**.

If we want the boldest and most experimental version, choose **Direction C: Product Worlds**.

## Suggested information architecture

No final structure yet, but the likely content model is:

- Homepage
- Selected work
- One page about how you think
- Writing/notes or technical reflections
- About
- Contact

Inside selected work, each case study should likely include:

- problem/context
- role and scope
- product lens
- architecture lens
- key technical choices
- obstacles and tradeoffs
- measurable outcome
- reflection

## Open design questions for the next phase

- How serious vs expressive should the site feel?
- How much personality should come from writing vs visuals vs interaction?
- Should the portfolio feel more like a lab, a field manual, or a set of worlds?
- Do we want a warm, tactile aesthetic or a sharper futuristic one?
- How much do you want to foreground research/publishing credibility?
